Trump Administration Agency Cases

Challenges to Agency Restructuring and Personnel Policies

Schedule F Executive Order Litigation

In October 2020, President Trump issued Executive Order 13957 creating a new "Schedule F" employment category that would have reclassified thousands of career civil servants into positions with fewer job protections. While the Biden administration revoked this order, there are ongoing concerns about its potential reinstatement in a future administration.

Key Legal Issues:

  • Presidential authority over the civil service system established by Congress
  • Statutory interpretation of the Civil Service Reform Act
  • Constitutional separation of powers
  • Administrative Procedure Act requirements for policy changes

Bureau of Land Management Headquarters Relocation Challenges

The Trump administration's relocation of the Bureau of Land Management (BLM) headquarters from Washington DC to Grand Junction, Colorado led to significant staff departures and organizational disruption. This move was challenged as an attempt to undermine the agency's effectiveness.

Key Legal Issues:

  • Authority for agency reorganization
  • Administrative Procedure Act requirements for major organizational changes
  • Congressional appropriations authority and limitations
  • Impact on statutory mission fulfillment

EPA Science Advisory Board Reconstitution

The Trump EPA's policy barring EPA grant recipients from serving on science advisory committees was challenged as arbitrary and capricious. This policy resulted in removing many academic scientists from advisory roles while increasing industry representation.

Key Legal Issues:

  • Requirements for reasoned explanation of policy changes
  • Statutory mandate for balanced committee composition
  • Federal Advisory Committee Act requirements
  • Conflict of interest standards

Regulatory Rollback Litigation

Clean Power Plan Repeal and Replacement Cases

The Trump EPA's repeal of the Clean Power Plan and replacement with the Affordable Clean Energy rule sparked multiple lawsuits. The DC Circuit ultimately vacated the ACE Rule, but the Supreme Court's West Virginia v. EPA decision later limited EPA authority in this area under the Major Questions Doctrine.

Key Legal Issues:

  • Major Questions Doctrine application
  • Agency authority to interpret statutory provisions
  • Requirements for reasoned explanation when changing policies
  • State roles in cooperative federalism schemes

View DC Circuit opinion on CourtListener

DACA Rescission and Reinstatement Litigation

The Trump administration's attempt to rescind the Deferred Action for Childhood Arrivals (DACA) program was invalidated by the Supreme Court in Department of Homeland Security v. Regents of the University of California for failing to consider important aspects of the problem (a classic State Farm violation). Subsequent litigation regarding DACA's legality continues.

Key Legal Issues:

  • Arbitrary and capricious review standards
  • Requirement to consider reliance interests
  • Procedural requirements for rescinding policies
  • Agency discretion in enforcement priorities

View Supreme Court opinion on CourtListener

Waters of the United States (WOTUS) Rule Changes

The Trump administration's Navigable Waters Protection Rule significantly narrowed the definition of "waters of the United States" protected under the Clean Water Act. This rule was later vacated by courts, and a new rule was issued by the Biden administration, continuing a multi-administration ping-pong on this definition.

Key Legal Issues:

  • Statutory interpretation of Clean Water Act terms
  • Precedential value of prior Supreme Court decisions (Rapanos)
  • Scientific evidence requirements for policy changes
  • State versus federal regulatory authority

Recent and Pending Supreme Court Administrative Law Cases

Major Recent Supreme Court Administrative Law Decisions

SEC v. Jarkesy (2024)

This case challenges the Securities and Exchange Commission's use of in-house administrative law judges to hear enforcement cases. The Supreme Court will decide whether this practice violates the Seventh Amendment right to a jury trial and raises other constitutional concerns about how ALJs are appointed and overseen.

Key Legal Issues:

  • Seventh Amendment right to jury trial in administrative proceedings
  • Constitutionality of agency adjudication structures
  • Non-delegation concerns regarding Congressional authorization of agency enforcement
  • Due process in administrative adjudication

View case docket on CourtListener

Loper Bright Enterprises v. Raimondo (2024)

This landmark decision overturned Chevron deference, ending nearly four decades of judicial deference to reasonable agency interpretations of ambiguous statutes. The Court held that judges must exercise their independent judgment to determine the best interpretation of a statute.

Key Legal Issues:

  • Judicial review of agency statutory interpretations
  • Separation of powers in administrative state
  • Stare decisis considerations in overturning longstanding precedent
  • Role of agency expertise in statutory interpretation

View opinion on CourtListener

Corner Post v. Board of Governors of the Federal Reserve System (2023)

The Supreme Court held that federal courts cannot grant deference to agency interpretations first advanced in litigation briefs. While not directly addressing Chevron or Kisor deference, this case further restricted deference to agencies.

Key Legal Issues:

  • Limits on deference to agency interpretations
  • Requirements for agency procedures when interpreting statutes
  • Consistency in agency positions during litigation
  • Relationship between briefing positions and official agency action

Pending Supreme Court Administrative Law Cases

Consumer Financial Protection Bureau v. Community Financial Services Association

This case challenges the CFPB's funding structure, which comes from the Federal Reserve rather than annual congressional appropriations. The Fifth Circuit held this violated the Appropriations Clause, potentially threatening the agency's existence.

Key Legal Issues:

  • Constitutional requirements for agency funding
  • Appropriations Clause interpretation
  • Congressional power to establish alternative funding mechanisms
  • Relationship between funding and executive control

View case docket on CourtListener

Ohio v. EPA

This case challenges EPA's authority to regulate interstate air pollution under the "Good Neighbor" provision of the Clean Air Act. It raises questions about EPA's modeling methods and the boundaries of federal authority over state implementation plans.

Key Legal Issues:

  • Chevron replacement doctrine application
  • Agency use of models and technical expertise
  • Cooperative federalism boundaries
  • Arbitrary and capricious review of technical decisions

Significant Circuit Court Administrative Law Cases

Recent Circuit Court Decisions Shaping Administrative Law

State of Missouri v. Biden (8th Cir. 2023)

This case involves challenges to alleged federal government "censorship" through communications with social media platforms about COVID-19 misinformation. It raises questions about government "jawboning" as a form of administrative action.

Key Legal Issues:

  • What constitutes "agency action" under the APA
  • Relationship between informal pressure and formal regulation
  • First Amendment limitations on agency communications
  • Standing to challenge informal agency influence

View opinion on CourtListener

Mexican Gulf Fishing Company v. NOAA (5th Cir. 2023)

This decision rejected NOAA Fisheries' vessel monitoring requirements for charter fishing boats. It represents a significant application of the Major Questions Doctrine at the circuit court level post-West Virginia v. EPA.

Key Legal Issues:

  • Application of Major Questions Doctrine to agency regulations
  • Scope of agency authority under organic statutes
  • Fourth Amendment considerations in administrative searches
  • Privacy interests versus regulatory needs

View opinion on CourtListener

Behring Regional Center v. DHS (9th Cir. 2023)

This case addresses the validity of a DHS rule issued by an Acting Secretary whose appointment was questioned under the Federal Vacancies Reform Act, highlighting increasing scrutiny of acting officials in administrative agencies.

Key Legal Issues:

  • Federal Vacancies Reform Act requirements
  • Validity of actions taken by improperly appointed officials
  • Remedies for violations of appointment procedures
  • Ratification of actions by properly appointed successors
